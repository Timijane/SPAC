Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our counseling services and process.

Is counseling really confidential?
Yes, absolutely. All counseling sessions are completely confidential, with exceptions only as required by law (risk of harm to self or others, child/elder abuse). We take your privacy seriously and adhere to strict ethical guidelines.
How does Christian counseling differ from secular counseling?
Christian counseling integrates psychological principles with biblical truth. We address the whole person—spiritual, emotional, mental, and relational—recognizing that true healing comes through both therapeutic techniques and spiritual growth in Christ.
How long does counseling typically last?
This varies based on individual needs. Some issues may be addressed in 6-8 sessions, while deeper concerns or marriage counseling may take several months. We work at your pace and regularly assess progress toward your goals.
What if my spouse won't come to marriage counseling?
You can still benefit from individual counseling to work on your part of the relationship dynamics. Often, when one partner begins to make positive changes, it can encourage the other to join. We also offer resources you can use at home.
Do you accept insurance?
Our licensed counselors can provide receipts for insurance reimbursement. Our pastoral counselors offer services on a sliding scale based on income. We believe finances should not be a barrier to receiving help.
